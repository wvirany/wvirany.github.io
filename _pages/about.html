I'm a BS/MS student at the University of Colorado Boulder studying applied mathematics with an emphasis in computer science and a minor in physics. My general interests lie in data science and machine learning, specifically with applications in medicine and biology. I am also interested in deep learning; both theory and applications.

Recently, I have been exploring the application of machine learning to the metagenomic analysis of the human gut microbiome. Models which can predict disease earlier and more accuractely have the potential to revolutionize P4 medicine (personalized, preventive, predictive, participatory). Additionally, I'm currently working on a project to tackle the ongoing question in deep learning; why do deep neural networks generalize so well? I am exploring the nature of approximation with deep neural networks in an attempt to better explain their generalizability.
